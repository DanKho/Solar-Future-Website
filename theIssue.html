So, what's the issue?

Global Climate Change has been a topic of heated debate and controversy in society since the late 19th century. The Earth’s climate has been changing consistently throughout history. According to NASA, just in the last 650,000 years, there have been multiple cycles of glacier advance and retreat, with the abrupt end of the last ice age about 11,700 years ago marking the beginning of the modern climate era - and of human civilization. Some argue that the current trends in rising global temperature, warming oceans, shrinking ice sheets, and rising sea levels are all part of the same natural cycle which lacks any direct correlation with human activity. The latest scientific research, however, indicates just the opposite. It is evident that the Earth’s average surface temperature has risen about 0.9 degrees Celsius since the late 19th century, a change largely driven by increased carbon dioxide and other man-made emissions into the atmosphere. Alarmingly, this seemingly insignificant shift in the planet’s global temperature can lead to a potentially devastating series of events including, but not limited to flooding and erosion of coastal and low lying areas, extreme droughts and wildfires, reduced agricultural yields, as well as declining freshwater supplies around the world. The evidence also suggests that the primary source of human-generated emissions involve the usage of economically efficient, yet highly unsustainable energy generation methods such as the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas for electricity, heat, and transportation. At Solar Future, we believe it is time for society to move on and widely adapt to newer, more sustainable electricity-generation technologies focused on harnessing useful energy directly from the Sun. We hope that this will ensure a brighter, long-term future for us and the many generations ahead.
